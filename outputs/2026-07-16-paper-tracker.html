LLM-Memory
Extending LLM Context via Associative Recurrent Memory
Kuzmin, Rodkin, Bulatov, Kuratov, Rvanova, Katkov, Sochenkov, Tsodyks, Baldwin, Burtsev, Shelmanov · arXiv, Jul 13 LLM-Memory KV-Networks

Approach: Construct domain-specific long-context datasets and a comprehensive training recipe for the Associative Recurrent Memory Transformer (ARMT): continued pre-training, synthetic long-context data generation, curriculum learning, and selective associative memory integration into chosen model layers. ARMT replaces standard quadratic self-attention with constant-memory recurrent associative memory.

Finding: ARMT-augmented models process inputs well beyond original context limits without degrading performance, generalize better to out-of-distribution lengths, and require 30% fewer FLOPs while preserving baseline performance within the original context window. Note: Misha Tsodyks (Weizmann Institute) is a co-author — a rare case of a computational neuroscientist contributing directly to LLM memory architecture.

Track, Rank, Crack: Epistemic Working Memory Scales Multi-Hop Reasoning
Ning Liu · arXiv, Jul 14 LLM-Memory

Approach: SLEUTH introduces explicit structured epistemic working memory with three components: Confirmed Facts (source-grounded), Active Hypotheses (evidence-ranked), and Open Questions (action-driving). Agent maintains this state across multi-hop reasoning chains, tested on five benchmarks against Reflexion and other baselines.

Finding: SLEUTH's advantage grows with difficulty: +5 points on HotpotQA, +11 on 4-hop chains. Enforcing protocol adherence on a weaker model recovers up to +19 points on hardest problems — organized epistemic state, not raw model capability, is the active ingredient for scaling multi-hop reasoning.

Speculate with Memory: Lossless Acceleration for LLM Agents
Li, Ye, Choubey, Zhang, Wu (Salesforce AI) · arXiv, Jul 14 LLM-Memory

Approach: Equip the LLM agent speculator with three online memory systems: (1) contrastive transition table tracking action-sequence statistics, (2) episodic memory retrieving contextually similar segments, and (3) confusion tracker suppressing recurring errors. All speculation is lossless — runs during idle time at zero added wall-clock cost.

Finding: Memory-augmented speculation yields 19–39% relative accuracy improvement on action prediction and up to 2.5× increase on observation prediction. Gains grow continuously as memory accumulates and generalize across speculator models of varying cost.

Knowledgeless Language Models: Suppressing Parametric Recall for Evidence-Grounded LM
Cohen, Carré, Lechtenbörger, Droste, Kerschke, Biswas, de Melo, Buys (HPI / U. Cape Town) · arXiv, Jul 14 LLM-Memory

Approach: Introduce Knowledge-"Less" Language Models (KLLMs), pretrained on corpora where named entities are anonymized to remove entity-linked factual supervision. Tested across multiple model scales on contextual QA, fact verification, hallucination detection, and retrieval-grounded benchmarks with imperfect evidence.

Finding: KLLMs substantially reduce closed-book factual recall while improving contextual task performance. In retrieval-grounded settings with imperfect evidence, KLLMs achieve 20–25% relative gains. Better calibration (ECE, Brier score, AUROC) and more reliable abstention. Suppressing entity-linked supervision during pretraining shifts from parametric recall toward evidence-grounded reasoning.

Data-Efficient Adaptation of LLMs via Attention Head Reweighting
Oikarinen, Chen, Siska, Weng, Singh, Gao (Microsoft Research) · COLM 2026 LLM-Memory

Approach: Attention Head Reweighting (AHR) learns only a single scalar per attention head — modifying ~0.0001% of model parameters. The insight: attention heads exhibit functional specialization, and reweighting them provides a surprisingly expressive adaptation mechanism. Evaluated on diverse text classification against LoRA and full fine-tuning baselines.

Finding: AHR outperforms LoRA on limited-sample regimes despite 200–1000× fewer trainable parameters. Learned head weights are directly interpretable, revealing which heads are responsible for in-context learning abilities — providing a window into the functional organization of transformer memory relevant to attention-based episodic memory mechanisms.

The Refusal Residue: When Probes Catch Alignment Faking and When They Don't
Aman Mehta · ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop LLM-Memory

Approach: 13-model sweep for naturally emerging alignment faking. Probes hidden states for a "refusal residue" distinguishing genuine from strategic compliance. Introduces rigorous five-control measurement framework: multi-token extraction, refuse-vs-refuse confound checks, per-fold residualization, leave-one-query-out evaluation, and orthogonality-constrained probing.

Finding: Natural faking appears in Qwen3-32B (+18.2pp) and Llama-3.1-8B (+24.4pp, p<10⁻¹⁵). Asymmetric refusal residue: monitored compliance shifts toward refusal (d=0.32–0.33, p<0.0001), refusal representations do not move (p=1.00). Detection is model-conditional; standard probing inflates AUROC by 0.2–0.3. Hidden-state memory of behavioral disposition exists but is fragile and easy to mis-measure.

⚠ Single-author paper; experimental scope limited to two models with detectable faking
What Models Express, Suppress, and Resist: Auditing Open-Weight LLMs with Persona Vectors
Zeng, Emami, Choi (Emory University) · arXiv, Jul 14 LLM-Memory

Approach: First systematic application of persona vectors — behavioral directions in activation space — at scale. 53-trait inventory across four domains, labeling every trait as natural, steerable, or intractable. Tests compositional steering with two vectors applied simultaneously.

Finding: Both models default to helpful, task-oriented behavior; all nine agentic traits are natural. Steering produces largest gains on excluded traits (hyperbole, hallucination, sycophancy). Key asymmetry: two steerable traits can collapse composition, but pairs involving a default never do. For intractable traits like "evil," a transferred vector from a fine-tuned variant recovers it, with residual refusals in chain-of-thought. Persona vectors are most informative as probes of behavioral organization, not control knobs.

Targeted Recovery of Weight-Space Mechanisms From Neural Networks
Vigouroux, Sharkey · ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop LLM-Memory

Approach: Targeted Parameter Decomposition (tPD) identifies only components that process specific inputs by introducing a high-rank catch-all component for non-target data. Validated on toy models and transformer LMs trained on The Pile.

Finding: Extracts a CSS-only submodel of a 4-block transformer at 7% FLOPs of full decomposition. In a 12-block transformer, surgically ablates and rewires memorized sequences with negligible side effects — directly relevant to isolating weight-space circuits responsible for memorization in lingering memory experiments.

Graded Entity-Familiarity Readouts in Language Models
Grzegorz Brzezinka · arXiv, Jul 15 LLM-Memory

Approach: Studies activations at the final prompt token in 12 instruction-tuned models (Bielik, PLLuM, Gemma-4, Qwen3 families) using 1,440 Polish entities spanning four domains and ten Wikipedia-pageview deciles plus fabricated controls. Tests cross-language robustness.

Finding: Familiarity-probe scores separate real from fabricated entities in every family. Polish-adapted models track entity popularity (ρ=0.28–0.57 vs. ≤0.11 in non-Polish). 1D familiarity direction at a single layer moves refusal rates monotonically: 0.24→1.00 on known, 0.73→0.00 on unknown. Graded pre-generation readout separates representational familiarity from the policy converting it into abstention — analogous to memory strength vs. retrieval criterion distinction.

Schema-Episodic
gradCSCG: Differentiable Clone-Structured Causal Graphs for End-to-End Cognitive Map Learning
Nikzad, Sarbishegi, Dasmeh, Asif, Gharavi, Husom, Sen, Lehr, Penacchio, Clemente, Stöber · arXiv, Jul 14 Schema-Episodic Cross-cutting

Approach: Reformulate the Clone-Structured Causal Graph (CSCG) — a normative hippocampus model for cognitive maps — as a fully differentiable module (gradCSCG) coupled to a VQ-VAE perceptual front-end. Soft emission forward pass allows map-learning objective to flow back into perception. Tested on symbolic grid worlds and MNIST image sequences across four aliased environments.

Finding: On MNIST image sequences — where each location visit yields a randomly sampled digit — the end-to-end pipeline uncovers the underlying adjacency graph with high edge precision and recall. Proof-of-principle that a hippocampus-inspired cognitive map model can serve as a composable building block in deep learning architectures.

⚠ Preprint; no biological validation against neural data — purely computational proof-of-concept.
Activity Regeneration from Silent States in Neuronal Networks with Transient Synaptic Memory
Khanjanianpak, Valiadeh (IASBS, Iran) · arXiv, Jul 15 Schema-Episodic KV-Networks

Approach: Minimal neuronal network model with finite-lifetime synapses. Investigates whether residual synaptic state alone predicts future network dynamics after activity ceases. Analyzes the "synaptic-memory snapshot" at the first silent state to determine single-cycle vs. multi-cycle dynamics.

Finding: Latent Excitatory Recruitment (LER) capacity — cumulative number of fresh excitatory neurons from the synaptic snapshot — near-perfectly predicts multi-cycle regeneration. Distinct outcomes emerge in homogeneous networks, demonstrating transient synaptic memory alone generates diverse future dynamics. The concept of a static memory snapshot encoding retrieval capacity maps cleanly onto KV memory network frameworks.

KV-Networks
How Query Visibility Changes KV-Cache Compression Rankings: A Matched-Budget Audit
Luo, Liang, Xuan · arXiv, Jul 11 KV-Networks

Approach: Matched-budget audit of six published compression methods against three trivial baselines on three open 7–9B models, with 144,300 paired evaluations on RULER-8192 and 40,800 on LongBench — all under both query-aware and query-agnostic protocols.

Finding: Query visibility changes rankings dramatically. Under agnostic protocol, only KeyDiff beats trivial baselines consistently (31/36 cells). SnapKV loses to "keep start + recent window" on average (Δ=−0.066). Per-method protocol drop is ordered by how visible the query is to each method's scoring signal — a sobering audit every KV-cache paper should be required to pass.

Forgetful Attention: A Trainable Support-Vector Memory with Certified Selection and Exact Unlearning
Vishwajith Ramesh · arXiv, Jul 13 KV-Networks LLM-Memory

Approach: Reframe attention as an online learner over context, with weights from a one-class SVM (fixed box parameter C). Active-set partition gives reserve tokens exactly zero weight, certifying output-preserving eviction. Reversible incremental solver deletes a token to recover retrained state. Tested on MIMIC-IV medical streams, enwik8, and TinyStories.

Finding: Certified selection: 0.86 vs. 0.32 rare-item recall, 0.80 vs. 0.05 deterioration hours on MIMIC-IV. On enwik8: 2.178 BPC vs. 2.383 for matched-state sliding-window Transformer (8.6% improvement, p=0.001). Reversible solver recovers identical partitions with median deviation ~10⁻⁹.

A JoLT for the KV Cache: Near-Lossless Compression via Tucker + JL-Residual
Krishnan, Schulz · arXiv, Jul 14 KV-Networks

Approach: KV cache as third-order tensor (heads × tokens × features) with different redundancy across axes. Partial Tucker decomposition compresses token and feature axes while leaving head and layer axes intact. Johnson-Lindenstrauss rotated low-bit residual restores discarded energy. Lagrangian dual allocates ranks and bit-widths jointly.

Finding: Near-lossless 2–3× compression: perplexity, GSM8K, and RULER retrieval all within statistical noise of uncompressed baseline on Mistral-7B and LLaMA-2-13B. At 2×: Frobenius error 0.009 (K) and 0.006 (V) — roughly order of magnitude below cross-layer SVD and 4-bit quantization. FlashJoLT variant: 5–13× compression-time speedup.

Adaptive Filtering of the KV Cache: Diagnosing and Correcting Structural-Role Bias in LLM Inference
Soumil Mandal · arXiv, Jul 14 KV-Networks LLM-Memory

Approach: Diagnoses a fundamental problem in attention-based KV cache eviction (H2O and descendants): on schema-dense inputs like nested JSON, structural tokens (delimiters, whitespace) carry an order of magnitude more attention mass than content tokens. Proposes retraining-free, role-conditional allocation using a 15 MB linear role probe labeling tokens as KEY (structural) or VALUE (content-carrying).

Finding: At 5% budget, structural KEY tokens are over-retained at ~1.8× rate of VALUE tokens, collapsing exact-match accuracy from 88% to 0%. Suppressing KEY tokens closes 63–98% of the H2O gap at sub-20% budgets. This is a critical finding: attention mass — the dominant signal for memory retention in transformers — is contaminated by structural noise unrelated to content, analogous to the position-preference bias in the lingering memory experiments.

Encoding-Retrieval
Comparing Semantic Navigation in Humans and Large Language Models using NLP
Paris-Colombo, Cabral-Carvalho, Toro-Hernández · arXiv, Jul 13 (CogSci 2026) Encoding-Retrieval Cross-cutting

Approach: Compare retrieval dynamics between 82 human participants and three LLMs (GPT-4o, Gemini-2.5-Pro, Claude-Sonnet-4.5) across eight temperature settings using verbal fluency data. Three trajectory-based NLP metrics: entropy (step-size predictability), distance to next (successive semantic steps), and distance to centroid (global dispersion).

Finding: Humans exhibited higher entropy, larger semantic steps, and broader dispersion than all LLMs — more variable and exploratory search. Temperature tuning produced only partial alignments; no configuration reproduced the complete human retrieval profile. Human semantic search implements a distinctive balance between local exploitation and global exploration that current transformers fail to reproduce.

Cross-cutting
Constructed Reality, Contested Priors: Cognitive Relapse Under the Free Energy Principle
MD Ibrahim Hossain Ridoy · arXiv, Jul 12 (q-bio.NC cross-list) Cross-cutting Schema-Episodic

Approach: Convolutional VAE with recurrent latent predictor (ELBO ≡ variational free energy), trained first on baseline domain, then on mixed stream with swept rehearsal ratio r controlling baseline persistence. Representational capacity (discrimination) tracked separately from default behavior (unconstrained generation) across 90 runs.

Finding: Representational accuracy stays near ceiling (0.97–0.998) regardless of r, while default behavior spans nearly the system's entire range — a sharp decoupling of learning from acceptance. At intermediate r, the system's default output rises toward target, then partially reverts toward baseline while training continues — "cognitive relapse." Implications for schema updating and why systems resist incorporating new priors even when they demonstrably "know" new information.

⚠ Single-author preprint; VAE-as-FEP mapping is a modeling choice, not empirical demonstration.
Local Redundancy: An Information-Theoretic Measure of Plasticity from Synthetic Memorization
Jiaxuan Cheng · ICML 2026 Spotlight Cross-cutting

Approach: Introduces local redundancy — an information-theoretic measure of plasticity from universal compression theory. Defined as worst-case redundancy of a local model family along gradient directions. Proves expected squared gradient norm on a synthetic memorization task provides an efficiently computable lower bound. Validated on continual image classification and time series transfer learning.

Finding: Local redundancy predicts downstream performance better than existing plasticity measures (effective rank, dead neuron fraction, weight norm) and enables pretraining checkpoint selection where validation loss plateaus. Connects abstract plasticity concept to a computable quantity from memorization dynamics — using controlled memorization probes to characterize memory architecture, mirroring the logic of lingering memory experiments.

Do Agent Optimizers Compound? A Continual-Learning Evaluation on Terminal-Bench 2.0
Wang, Kattakinda, Feizi (UMD / RELAI) · arXiv, Jul 15 Cross-cutting

Approach: Two-phase continual-learning evaluation testing whether optimizer gains compound when applied recursively as new tasks appear. Compares GEPA, Meta Harness, and RELAI-VCL under identical budgets on Terminal-Bench 2.0.

Finding: All three improve in static single-phase setting, but diverge sharply with new tasks: GEPA transfers below baseline, Meta Harness transfers well but fails to improve further, only RELAI-VCL both transfers positively and continues improving (76.4% vs. 58.7% baseline). Active ingredient: regression control providing inductive bias against shortcut solutions. Directly relevant to model-iterator workflow — single-phase optimization gains may be illusory when facing new inputs.

Peripheral
Sparse Inter-Layer Dependencies of Transformer FFN Neurons
Knittel, Pfister (Harvard) · arXiv, Jul 13 Peripheral

Approach: Training-free attribution method estimating relative influence of upstream FFN neurons and attention outputs on a target neuron's activation. Neuron-specific masking propagated through the full network.

Finding: Small subsets of preceding activations suffice to preserve neuron outputs with high fidelity. When masks are applied simultaneously across all layers, perplexity remains largely unchanged at moderate sparsity. FFNs exhibit sparse and structured inter-layer dependencies despite dense parameterization.

Grounded World Models in Biological Organisms and Future Embodied AI
Pezzulo, Nuzzi, D'Alessandro, Proietti, Bottini, Cisek (ISTC-CNR Rome / U. Montreal) · arXiv, Jul 15 Peripheral

Approach: Perspective contrasting biological and artificial world model construction. Illustrates five neural circuits for grounded world modelling: navigation, affordance-based perception, active perception, allostatic control, and self-world distinction. Argues biology builds grounded models through interaction first, with language as later scaffold.

Finding: Identifies features missing from embodied AI: intrinsic dynamics as learning foundation, action centrality, autonomous open-ended learning, and early predictive mechanisms scaffolding higher cognition. Theoretical scaffolding for why hippocampal-like mechanisms may be necessary rather than optional for systems that need to remember temporally structured experience.
