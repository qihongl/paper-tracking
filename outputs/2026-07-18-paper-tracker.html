MemPoison: Uncovering Persistent Memory Threats and Structural Blind Spots in LLM Agents
Jifeng Gao, Kang Xia, Yi Zhang, Xiaobin Hong et al. · arXiv:2607.14651 — 16 July 2026 LLM-Memory

Approach: Proposes a benchmark and analysis framework (1,227 hand-validated cases across four attack types, three injection channels, three memory substrates) evaluated on seven open-weight and three closed-weight model families. Defines a three-tier taxonomy: L1 direct single-record corruption, L2 compositional multi-record corruption, L3 context-triggered dormant corruption.

Finding: Persistent external memory — the very capability that gives LLM agents continuity across turns — is a durable attack surface: adversarial content injected through ordinary interaction channels is retained and later distorts downstream behavior. Quantifies how memory persistence (a Pillar-1 capability) creates new, persistent safety failure modes that static safety filters miss.

Bridge Evidence: Static Retrieval Utility Does Not Predict Causal Utility in Multi-Step Agentic Search
Debayan Mukhopadhyay, Utshab Kumar Ghosh, Shubham Chatterjee · arXiv:2607.15253 — 16 July 2026 LLM-Memory

Approach: Measures the gap between static retrieval utility (does a document, read in isolation, improve the answer?) and causal utility (does it matter for what the agent does next?) using a ReAct-style agent over HotpotQA. Replays 1,000 development questions, deleting each read document and re-running the remainder of the trajectory.

Finding: Static retrieval metrics systematically fail to predict a document's causal contribution in multi-step agentic search — a direct methodological caution for evaluating LLM memory/retrieval systems: offline retrieval scores can mislead about what an agent actually uses.

Schema-Episodic
Goal-directed hippocampal theta sweeps during memory-guided navigation
Wenbo Tang, Xiyu Mei, Ryan E. Harvey, Estrella Carbajal-Leon, Talia Netzer, Hongyu Chang, Azahara Oliva, Antonio Fernandez-Ruiz · Nature Neuroscience — 1 July 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Recorded hippocampal population activity as rodents navigated tasks in which the hidden-goal configuration changed; used internal-map switching and Bayesian decoding of single theta-cycle sweeps to test whether sweeps target remembered goals rather than mere sensory/motor sampling.

Finding: Hippocampal activity sweeps toward remembered goals on internal cognitive maps, independent of immediate sensory input; goal-directed sweeps coordinate with prefrontal cortex and are preferentially replayed offline. Provides a substrate for both online and offline memory-guided planning. (Back-to-back with Yu et al., same issue.)

Hippocampal theta sweeps indicate goal direction during navigation
Changmin Yu, Zilong Ji, Jake Ormond, John O'Keefe, Neil Burgess · Nature Neuroscience — 1 July 2026 (Open Access) Schema-Episodic

Approach: Used a 'Honeycomb' maze that dissociates head-, movement-, and goal-direction, recorded CA1 place-cell ensembles, fit a theta-sweep model with an added goal-directed input, and tested its prediction of goal-phase precession empirically.

Finding: Theta sweeps show a robust goal-oriented directional bias independent of movement or head direction (p = 0.0004 vs. movement direction); replay during sharp-wave ripples is also goal-directed. Demonstrates theta sweeps are a neural substrate for online goal-directed planning, not motor sampling. (Companion to Tang et al.)

Human hippocampal ripples tune cortical responses based on predicted uncertainty
Darya Frank, Stephan Moratti, ... Bryan A. Strange (with K. Friston) · Nature Neuroscience — 25 June 2026 (Open Access) Schema-Episodic

Approach: Direct human intracranial recordings (hippocampus + visual cortex) during a visual task with varying predictive uncertainty; analyzed pre-stimulus ripples, fusiform gamma, the aperiodic exponent (E/I balance), and Granger causality.

Finding: Hippocampal ripples increase ahead of unpredictable events and tune visual cortex to respond faster and stronger to surprising stimuli; pre-stimulus ripples shift fusiform E/I balance toward controlled inhibition (precision-weighting), and post-stimulus gamma drives bottom-up error flow back to hippocampus. First direct human evidence that ripples implement predictive-coding precision-weighting in waking perception, not only sleep consolidation.

⚠ ⚠ Human iEEG — small participant N (typical for intracranial studies); ripple-timing/causal claims rest on correlational analyses.
A learning-evoked slow-oscillatory architecture paces population activity for offline reactivation across the human medial temporal lobe
Adrien A. Causse, Julie Curot, Vitor Lopes-Dos-Santos, ... David Dupret, Helen C. Barron · Neuron — 2026 (ePub ahead of print) Schema-Episodic

Approach: Human intracranial recordings combining single-neuron spiking and local field potentials across the medial temporal lobe during learning and post-learning rest; quantified slow-oscillatory bursts, cross-regional gamma coordination, and ripple-reactivation of learning-evoked motifs.

Finding: Mnemonic engagement elicits on-demand slow-oscillatory bursts in the hippocampus that synchronize gamma across MTL regions; these learning-evoked motifs are selectively reactivated during hippocampal ripples at rest, and the strength of their reactivation predicts subsequent recall accuracy. Identifies a multi-scale coordination mechanism linking learning, consolidation, and recall in humans.

⚠ ⚠ Human iEEG — limited participant count; reactivation–recall link is correlational.
KV-Networks
Long-Context Fine-Tuning with Limited VRAM
Vladimir Fedosov, Aleksandr Sazhin, Artemiy Grinenko, Frank Woernle · arXiv:2607.15105 — 16 July 2026 KV-Networks

Approach: Combines Hierarchical Global Attention (HGA) with segment-wise backpropagation and tiered KV storage: only the active segment stays differentiable in VRAM, older KV is detached to RAM/NVMe, and HGA loads a bounded set of exact historical tokens per query block. Evaluated on Qwen3-8B with 4-bit QLoRA on PG19.

Finding: On a 16 GB Quadro RTX 5000, dense training fits 2,048 tokens but fails at 4,096; HGA reaches 16,384 tokens at 15.28 GB peak VRAM and runs the same adapter through 131,072 tokens. A concrete KV-memory tiering scheme — a bounded, hierarchical key-value store — directly relevant to Pillar 3.

LongStraw: Long-Context RL Beyond 2M Tokens under a Fixed GPU Budget
Changhai Zhou, Kieran Liu, Yuhua Zhou, Qian Qiao et al. · arXiv:2607.14952 — 16 July 2026 KV-Networks

Approach: An architecture-aware execution stack for million-token RL post-training (GRPO) under a fixed GPU budget: evaluates the shared prompt without autograd, retains only model-specific state needed by later tokens, and replays short response segments. Targets agent trajectories where observations, tool outputs, and prior decisions accumulate.

Finding: Enables long-context RL post-training beyond 2M tokens within a fixed budget by decoupling prompt recomputation from response gradients — directly attacking the memory-scaling bottleneck for long-horizon agent memory (Pillar 3: key-value state that must persist and be queried across a long trajectory).

VTM-Nav: Hierarchical Visual-Topological Memory for Cross-Episode Object-Goal Navigation
Xiaoran Xu, Yupeng Wu, Tianyu Xue, Yifan Xu et al. · arXiv:2607.14514 — 16 July 2026 KV-Networks

Approach: Introduces Cross-Episode Object-Goal Navigation (an agent repeatedly operates in the same scene, parameters fixed, retaining only self-acquired experience) and VTM-Nav, a training-free VLM framework with a persistent hierarchical Visual-Topological Memory (VTM).

Finding: A persistent, structured memory that accumulates and reuses experience across episodes improves object-goal navigation without fine-tuning — a concrete instantiation of experience replay / memory consolidation in embodied agents (Pillar 3: memory that persists and is queried across episodes, with hierarchical structure).

Peripheral
Memory-Driven Self-Disclosure and Relational Turning Points: A Longitudinal Multimodal Study of Human-AI Interaction
Ryuichi Sumida, Mao Saeki, Masaki Eguchi, Sadahiro Yoshikawa et al. · arXiv:2607.14593 — 16 July 2026 Peripheral

Approach: Longitudinal study of a memory-augmented conversational agent (24 participants × 10 sessions); after each session participants rated familiarity, self-disclosure, perceived memory, conversational quality, and enjoyment.

Finding: Perceived memory is relationally conditioned — predicted by prior relational state rather than reflecting content — and is a key driver of long-term relationship formation. An HCI angle on memory-augmented LLMs, relevant to Pillar 1's user-memory framing (how a persistent memory changes the relational dynamic).

Online Neural Space Time Memory for Dynamic Novel View Synthesis
Baback Elmieh, Lynn Tsai, Zeman Li, Srinivas Kaza et al. · arXiv:2607.15271 — 16 July 2026 Peripheral

Approach: Proposes decoupling memory-update frequency from memory-application frequency in Test-Time-Training for online novel-view synthesis from streaming video, maintaining a persistent long-horizon memory under strict real-time constraints.

Finding: Infrequent, decoupled memory updates preserve long-horizon reconstruction of temporarily occluded regions while meeting real-time constraints — a gated persistent-state memory idea with a loose analogy to hippocampal gating. Peripheral to the three pillars but a clean mechanism sketch.
