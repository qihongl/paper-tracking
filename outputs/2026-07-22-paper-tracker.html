LLM-Memory
Copy Less, Ground More: Overcoming Repetitive Copying in Long-Context Reasoning via Evidence-Aware Reinforcement Learning
Lizhe Fang, Weizhou Shen, Tianyi Tang, Yisen Wang · arXiv cs.CL · 2026-07-21 LLM-Memory

Approach: They identify a 'repetitive copying' failure mode in long-context LLMs, where models copy input text verbatim into their reasoning traces instead of solving. They decompose each prompt into task-relevant key evidence versus irrelevant distractor context and train an evidence-aware RL objective that rewards grounding the answer in key evidence rather than wholesale copying; evaluated across several frontier long-context LLMs.

Finding: Repetitive copying is pervasive and intensifies with context length; the root cause is insufficient grounding in distractor-rich contexts. The evidence-aware RL reduces copying and improves reasoning on long-context benchmarks. Directly relevant to Pillar 1 (how attention uses long context and what survives under distraction).

⚠ Mostly an RL/engineering result; the 'grounding' claim is descriptive, not a causal attention-mechanism analysis. Preprint, not peer-reviewed.
Mark, Don't Erase: Token Inoculation for Dual-Use Knowledge in LLMs
Seunghyun Lee, Dongyoon Han, Sangdoo Yun · arXiv cs.CL · 2026-07-21 LLM-Memory

Approach: Rather than unlearning or erasing hazardous dual-use knowledge, they bind a special control token to hazardous content during continued pre-training, then behaviorally gate it at inference (conditioning, not reduction). They test retained adjacent-domain competence and over-refusal relative to erasure and refusal-training baselines.

Finding: Hazardous knowledge can be retained yet behaviorally gated by the privileged token, avoiding both the competence tax of erasure and the over-refusal of refusal training. Relevant to Pillar 1 as a memory-editing/forgetting mechanism (gating vs. deletion of stored knowledge).

⚠ Safety-oriented; the 'memory' framing is knowledge gating, not episodic memory. Preprint, not peer-reviewed.
Schema-Episodic
Adolescent exposure to the psychedelic 25C-NBOMe in rats induces lasting competitive avoidance through disrupted hippocampal-prefrontal synchrony
Yu, Zhang, Li, Hu et al. · Nature Neuroscience · 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Repeated adolescent (but not adult) exposure to the serotonergic psychedelic 25C-NBOMe in male rats; multisite recordings probed default-mode-network (DMN) synchrony, specifically theta-band coherence between ventral hippocampus and prefrontal cortex during social behavior.

Finding: Adolescent exposure produced lasting competitive avoidance and disrupted DMN synchrony, with reduced theta coherence between ventral hippocampus and PFC. Implicates hippocampus–PFC synchrony in an enduring, social-behavior change — relevant to Pillar 2 (hippocampal–cortical interactions and schema-level control).

Hippocampal ripples initiate cortical dimensionality expansion for memory retrieval
Kerrén, Michelmann, Doeller · Nature Communications · 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Intracranial EEG in patients with pharmacoresistant epilepsy during an episodic memory task; they analyzed cortical dimensionality around hippocampal ripple events during correct versus incorrect retrieval and measured hippocampal-theta / cortical-gamma phase-amplitude coupling.

Finding: Cortical dimensionality increased following ripple events during correct (but not incorrect) retrieval; this expansion correlated with faster reaction times and reinstatement of the target association. Hippocampal ripples initiate the low-dimensional→high-dimensional cortical expansion thought to reconstruct past experiences — direct human evidence for the ripple-driven reinstatement model (Pillar 2).

Myosin IIB Maintains Long-Term Memory
Silva, Marcondes, Lunardi, Popik et al. · Hippocampus · 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Pharmacological inhibition of myosin IIB (blebbistatin / p-nitro-blebbistatin) in hippocampus, amygdala, and insular cortex of rodents; tested fear-memory consolidation (post-training) and retrieval (pretest) with short- versus long-term memory assays.

Finding: Inhibiting myosin IIB in the hippocampus impairs long-term fear-memory consolidation but spares short-term memory; pretest blockade hinders retrieval; blocking 24 h after training impairs distinct memory phases. Positions myosin IIB — a dendritic-spine morphology protein — as necessary for both consolidation and retrieval (Pillar 2).

⚠ Rodent fear-conditioning; standard pharmacological-inhibition off-target caveats apply.
KV-Networks
Free energy landscape of Dense Associative Memory
Sumedha, Abhishek Singh · arXiv cs.AI · 2026-07-21 KV-Networks

Approach: Using large-deviations theory they derive a general free-energy functional for a broad class of associative memories (including dense associative memories), reproducing classical Hopfield results and deriving the temperature-dependent free energy for polynomial-interaction / Log-Sum-Exponential-activation dense networks; they evaluate the disorder-averaged ground-state energy in the extensive limit.

Finding: The framework shows how memory retrieval depends on the initial state in higher-order dense networks and yields exact retrieval-phase boundaries. A principled analytical handle on associative-memory retrieval dynamics — directly relevant to Pillar 3 (KV / associative memory networks, capacity vs. retrieval).

⚠ Preprint, not peer-reviewed; analytical result, not a learning simulation.
Encoding-Retrieval
When deliberate errors boost learning: Semantic constraints on the derring effect
Thomas, Mera, Higham, Seabrooke · J. Exp. Psychol.: Learning, Memory, & Cognition · 2026 Encoding-Retrieval

Approach: Four preregistered experiments; participants studied weakly related word pairs in errorful (generating deliberate errors) versus errorless conditions, then completed a cued-recall test; they tested semantic-relatedness constraints on the 'derring effect' under controlled conditions.

Finding: Deliberate-error generation benefited memory for complex information under specific semantic constraints (replicating and extending the derring effect), with theoretical resolution toward pretesting-like mechanisms. Relevant to retrieval practice / testing-effect and generation-based encoding (Pillar 1 / ER).

More memorable scenes and faces are learned better and forgotten more slowly
Ünal, Fiechter, Benjamin · J. Exp. Psychol.: Learning, Memory, & Cognition · 2026 Encoding-Retrieval

Approach: A continuous-recognition procedure (study events interleaved with test trials) plus a traditional study–test procedure; forgetting functions were estimated with a hierarchical Bayesian model accounting for subject, item, and retention-interval effects.

Finding: High-memorability images (faces and scenes) are both learned better and forgotten more slowly than low-memorability ones; the effect is item-driven rather than purely an encoding- or retrieval-stage difference. Relevant to item-level memorability and forgetting functions (ER).

Cholinergic inhibition disrupts retrieval-specific phosphorylation of CaMKII-α and CaMKII-β alongside ERK hyperactivation in the hippocampus during novel object recognition memory
Farrokhi, Askari, SoukhakLari, Moosavi · BMC Neuroscience · 2026 Encoding-Retrieval

Approach: In an Alzheimer's-disease model they assessed retrieval-specific molecular signaling in the hippocampus during novel-object recognition, measuring CaMKII-α/β phosphorylation and ERK activation under cholinergic inhibition.

Finding: Cholinergic inhibition disrupted retrieval-specific CaMKII phosphorylation while ERK was hyperactivated, linking cholinergic dysfunction to retrieval failure via divergent kinase signaling. Relevant to retrieval-specific molecular mechanisms (ER).

⚠ AD-pathology context; the mechanistic chain is correlational, not causal.
The Goal Bias Emerges During Motion Event Perception and Affects Language Production and Memory
Chen, Papafragou, Trueswell · Cognitive Science · 2026 Encoding-Retrieval

Approach: Eye-tracking during motion-event viewing and description, with later memory tested for Goals versus Sources; compared free-inspection versus description conditions to localize the Goal/Source asymmetry to online encoding.

Finding: An online attentional bias for Goals over Sources emerges during initial event encoding and predicts later memory (Goals are remembered more accurately). Connects event-encoding biases to subsequent memory (ER / event memory).

Cross-cutting
Serial dependence predicts generalization in perceptual learning
Pinchuk-Yacobi, Sagi, Bonneh · eLife · 2026 Cross-cutting

Approach: Reanalysis of >200,000 trials from a visual texture-discrimination task under three conditions that differentially modulate generalization; they quantified attractive serial-dependence effects (SDEs) and their temporal reach and persistence.

Finding: Short-term memory traces (SDEs) reached further back in time than previously reported and persisted after eight days of practice; SDE magnitude predicted learning generalization in conditions known to support it. Implicates temporal/sequential context (prior-trial traces) in long-term learning — relevant to Pillar 3 (temporal context models) and cross-cutting learning theory.
