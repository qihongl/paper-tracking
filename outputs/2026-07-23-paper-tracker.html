Schema-Episodic
A neural network model of free recall learns multiple memory strategies
Moufan Li, Kristopher T. Jensen, Qihong Lu, Marcelo G. Mattar (NYU / Columbia) · Nature Machine Intelligence, 20 Jul 2026 Schema-Episodic KV-Networks

Approach: Recurrent neural networks were trained end-to-end on a canonical free-recall task and then analyzed to recover the strategies they discovered, without being hand-coded with a temporal-context model (TCM).

Finding: The best models do not rely on pure TCM-style temporal chaining. Instead they learn an index-based retrieval mechanism resembling the method of loci / "memory palace," plus several other human-like strategies — showing free recall is subserved by a family of strategies, not a single chaining rule. Directly relevant to Pillar 3 (list memory) and to your free-recall / TCM modeling: it challenges TCM as the sole computational account and offers an explicit key→item retrieval alternative.

★ From your own group — Qihong Lu is a co-author (Nature Machine Intelligence version of the bioRxiv preprint 10.1101/2025.09.25.678592, already in the dedup store).
PKMζ-PKCι/λ double-knockout demonstrates atypical PKC is crucial for the persistence of hippocampal LTP and spatial memory
Panayiotis Tsokas, Changchi Hsieh, … Todd C. Sacktor (SUNY Downstate) · eLife, 22 Jul 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Double knockout of the two atypical PKCs — PKMζ and PKCι/λ — in the hippocampus, followed by electrophysiological measurement of late-phase LTP and behavioral assessment of long-term spatial memory.

Finding: When PKMζ (the canonical maintenance molecule) is removed, PKCι/λ becomes persistently active and still sustains late-LTP and long-term spatial memory. The result reveals compensatory redundancy among atypical PKCs in memory persistence — a mechanistic correction to the long-standing "PKMζ is necessary and sufficient" claim, and a concrete molecular substrate for the maintenance phase your Schema-Episodic RNN must ultimately reduce to.

Dyscoordination of thalamic reticular spindles is associated with social memory deficits in mice and humans with ASD
Dongqi Cui et al. (Chinese Academy of Sciences) · Nature Communications, 23 Jul 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Combined thalamic reticular nucleus (TRN) spindle recordings in mice with social-memory assays, spindle rescue manipulations, and analysis of sleep-spindle features in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD).

Finding: TRN spindle dyscoordination disrupts social-memory consolidation; restoring spindle coordination rescues the mouse deficit, and spindle-feature deviations predict ASD diagnosis in children. Establishes a thalamic sleep-spindle → memory-consolidation circuit with direct clinical translatability — a clean consolidation-mechanism paper for Pillar 2, complementary to the hippocampal-ripple consolidation literature.

Astrocytic α7 nicotinic acetylcholine receptors regulate glutamate dynamics and memory flexibility in the hippocampus
Tropea, Melone, Piacentini, Spiezio (Univ. of Naples) · Progress in Neurobiology, 21 Jul 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: α7-nAChR knockout mice plus an AAV astrocyte-specific re-expression model, examined with electron microscopy, glutamate/calcium/sodium imaging, electrophysiology, and behavioral flexibility tasks.

Finding: Astrocytic α7-nAChRs are abundant at CA1 excitatory synapses and gate glutamate uptake and synaptic plasticity; knockout impairs memory flexibility, and astrocyte-specific re-expression rescues it. Identifies a cholinergic–astrocyte–glutamate axis controlling hippocampal memory flexibility — a glial mechanism for the "schema-update / flexibility" defect your RNN is meant to capture.

Neural sampling from cognitive maps enables goal-directed imagination and planning
Hui Lin, Yukun Yang, Wolfgang Maass (TU Graz / IST Austria) · Nature Machine Intelligence, 21 Jul 2026 Schema-Episodic Cross-cutting

Approach: A brain-inspired generative model that represents a cognitive map, samples from it via stochastic computing and compositional coding, and learns using only local synaptic plasticity (no backprop through a world model).

Finding: The model performs goal-directed imagination and planning — two hallmark intelligence features — emerging directly from cognitive-map sampling. A neurally plausible alternative to the predictive-coding / world-model frameworks, and a concrete computational instantiation of the hippocampal cognitive-map → planning link central to Pillar 2.

KV-Networks
Question Tells You Where the Answer Is: Intention-aware Long-Context KV Cache Compression
Liang Zhao, Xiaocheng Feng, Bing Qin et al. (Harbin Institute of Technology) · ACL 2026 (Long Papers), July 2026 KV-Networks LLM-Memory

Approach: IntentKV is a query-aware KV-cache eviction method that first separates "intention tokens" — context tokens whose attention distribution reflects the user's semantic goal — from ordinary context tokens, then selects cache blocks by block-wise cumulative attention aggregated over intention tokens, reducing a 128K context to a 2K cache.

Finding: On diverse long-context benchmarks the method holds performance while cutting the KV cache from 128K→2K tokens, yielding 6.3× faster decoding and 7.8× better memory efficiency over the default setting. Directly on-pillar for KV memory networks (Pillar 3): it operationalizes which key–value entries survive as a function of the query/goal — the same query- and format-dependence question at the heart of your lingering-memory position/format confound (the 79% position-preference bias).

⚠ Applied compression-engineering (ACL 2026), no mechanistic-memory claim; included because it is the current KV-cache SOTA and bears directly on Pillar 3 key-retention.
HqeKV: Towards Hybrid Quantization and Eviction for KV Cache in Long-Context LLM Inference
He Wang, Yu Gu, Ge Yu et al. (Northeastern / CAS) · Findings of ACL 2026, July 2026 KV-Networks LLM-Memory

Approach: HqeKV combines both quantization and eviction (previous methods used one or the other) via an integrated optimizer that picks the best compression action per cached KV element, guided by a joint K–V importance metric and flexible multi-precision conversion.

Finding: Under identical memory constraints HqeKV improves generation quality over state-of-the-art compression alternatives, showing that treating keys and values with a single coarse importance score discards recoverable signal. A practical data point for Pillar 3: the key↔value asymmetry in importance (keys vs. values carry different memory-relevant information) is exactly the kind of structure a KV memory network should exploit when deciding what to retain across list/event/position memory.

⚠ Applied compression-engineering (Findings of ACL 2026), no mechanistic-memory claim; included as on-pillar Pillar 3 KV-cache work.

Cross-cutting
Two time scales of adaptation in human learning rates
Jonas Simoens, Senne Braem, Tom Verguts (Ghent) · eLife, 22 Jul 2026 Cross-cutting

Approach: Human behavioral task probing learning-rate adaptation, modeled with a fast and a slow adaptive timescale, combined with orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) measurement of the timescale representation.

Finding: People adapt learning rates on two dissociable timescales, and the central OFC selectively represents the slow timescale. Speaks directly to the neural-timescales literature your group tracks (Zeraati et al., Nature Neuroscience review, Run #8): it localizes a slow adaptive timescale to OFC, relevant to how temporal context and learning rate interact in both biological and artificial memory.

A 5.0 T Ultra-High-Field fMRI Dataset for Naturalistic Visual Scene Processing (Natural Vision Dataset, NVD)
Ye, Wang, Li, Peng · Scientific Data, 20 Jul 2026 Cross-cutting

Approach: 5.0 T fMRI from 20 healthy participants viewing a shared set of 1,268 natural images plus 500 participant-specific images (≈10,000 subject-specific images total), each presented three times — a static-naturalistic viewing design with shared stimulus anchors and repeated measures.

Finding: A public, naturalistic-viewing fMRI resource explicitly built for evaluating encoding and decoding models under stimulus diversity and repetition. Directly actionable for your ongoing naturalistic-replication projects (Sherlock / PIEMAN / Tunnel / OpenNeuro ds005658): the shared-image anchors and 3× repetition enable within- and between-subject alignment and reliability analyses out of the box.

Consensus-based Sharp-Wave Ripple detection and its application in an alcohol administration model
Ruelas, Fuentes-Aguilar, Medina-Ceja, Morales-Vargas · Alcohol (Alcohol Research), 2026 Cross-cutting

Approach: A consensus detector that first identifies sharp waves, then ripples within those intervals, labeling an event as a true SWR only when at least two independent detectors agree.

Finding: The consensus strategy improves SWR-detection reliability over either single detector alone (trading off recall vs. precision). A practical tool for the ripple-replay measurements at the heart of Pillar 2 — worth knowing about before your next iEEG/human-replay analysis, though validated here in an alcohol-administration context rather than a memory task.

⚠ Methods/benchmark paper, not a memory-mechanism finding; demonstrated in an alcohol model, so memory-relevance is indirect.
Peripheral
Linguistic structure and probability are jointly encoded in high gamma power
Slaats, Hervais-Adelman (Univ. of Zurich) · bioRxiv preprint, 22 Jul 2026 Peripheral

Approach: Intracranial EEG (70–150 Hz high-gamma) during speech comprehension, testing whether local cortical activity is modulated by lexical probability, syntactic structure, or both.

Finding: High-gamma power is jointly modulated by lexical probability and hierarchical syntactic structure — arguing against a purely statistical (LLM-style) account of structure building. Adjacent to your position/format and structure-vs-probability interests in lingering memory; the "structure vs. probability" dissociation is the kind of representational question worth tracking, even though it is language rather than memory per se.
