Hippocampal engrams configure prefrontal context representations to guide flexible decisions
Julian et al. (Tank) · bioRxiv, 2026-07-06 Schema-Episodic Cross-cutting

Approach: Combined activity tagging and optogenetic reactivation of hippocampal (HPC) engrams with simultaneous large-scale recordings of medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) in mice performing a context-dependent task-switching paradigm. This lets them test whether engram reactivation drives retrieval of an entire set of stimulus–response mappings rather than a single fixed response.

Finding: HPC engram reactivation reconfigured mPFC task representations to produce engram-consistent, flexible behavior—i.e., the engram specified which mapping set to use, not a single motor output. Direct evidence that hippocampal memory representations gate cortical computation online, squarely relevant to schema-guided episodic control of neocortical states.

The emergence of a novel CA1 spatial map requires direct entorhinal input
Chadney et al. (Dörr) · bioRxiv, 2026-07-02 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Population recordings of CA1 neurons paired with optogenetic silencing of their direct lateral-entorhinal input in mice exposed to a familiar and then a novel environment. Tests the relative contributions of intra-hippocampal vs. direct entorhinal drive to CA1 spatial remapping.

Finding: Silencing direct entorhinal input left spatial firing in the familiar environment intact but impaired remapping in the novel environment, producing a stable “hybrid” map blending both. Shows the direct entorhinal pathway is specifically recruited for CA1 novelty detection / new-map formation rather than maintenance of established maps.

Dynamics of Dentate Gyrus Place Cells and Dentate Spikes During Spatial and Nonspatial Changes in Environments
Demetrovich et al. (Colgin) · Journal of Neuroscience, 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: Extracellular recordings of DG place cells and local field potentials in rats of both sexes exploring a familiar environment subjected to discrete spatial and nonspatial (ethologically relevant) alterations, measuring how place-cell firing and dentate-spike dynamics track each manipulation.

Finding: DG place cells and dentate spikes are modulated by both spatial and nonspatial environmental changes, not solely spatial ones—extending the classical view of the DG as a pure pattern-separator for spatial context. Supports a broader role for dentate spikes in gating both spatial and nonspatial information into hippocampal codes.

Experience-Dependent Reorganization of Hippocampal CA3 Neuronal Ensembles Associates With Memory Generalization
Song et al. (Deng) · Advanced Science, 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: 8-arm-maze tasks in mice requiring transfer of a learned rule to novel configurations of varying difficulty; in vivo single-unit and population recordings of CA3 across two testing days tracked how ensemble representations reorganized with behavioral generalization.

Finding: CA3 single-unit and population trajectories distinguished easy task configurations on day 1 but not hard ones; as performance improved on day 2, representational separation sharpened selectively for the harder tasks. Generalization efficiency was inversely correlated with task difficulty, linking CA3 ensemble dynamics to memory generalization—directly germane to the generalization/defect questions in schema-bound RNN models.

Linking functional and structural dendritic spine remodeling during fear learning and extinction in vivo
Li et al. (Wong) · Science Advances, 2026 Schema-Episodic Cross-cutting

Approach: Longitudinal dual functional+structural in vivo imaging of frontal-association-cortex dendrites in mice across fear conditioning and extinction. A causal-inference model relates active spine calcium signals to spine elimination/consolidation.

Finding: Fear learning induced responsive spines that were more synchronous and clustered; these consolidated over following days but were attenuated by extinction. The model shows learning-era calcium signals protect spines from elimination while promoting elimination of neighbors—a mechanistic account of how dendritic integration encodes and later updates emotional memory traces.

Retrosplenial cortical reorganization during late adolescence introduces instability of contextual memory circuits
Zhang et al. (Wood) · PLOS Biology, 2026 Schema-Episodic Encoding-Retrieval

Approach: Tracked perineuronal-net (PNN) density and parvalbumin expression in mouse retrosplenial cortex (RSP) from early (p30) to late (p60–p75) adolescence alongside context-fear conditioning, with PNN-stabilization and -disruption manipulations.

Finding: RSP PNN/PV established at p30 declined by late adolescence, coinciding with impaired expression of early-adolescence-acquired contextual memories; both cellular and memory deficits were rescued by PNN stabilization and spontaneously recovered as PNNs rebuilt. Reveals a late-adolescent reorganization window that transiently destabilizes cortical memory circuits.

Impaired consolidation of spatial memory during sleep in patients with LGI1-associated limbic encephalitis
Rave et al. (Tabi) · Brain Communications, 2026 Schema-Episodic

Approach: 15 LGI1-antibody limbic-encephalitis patients and matched controls learned a virtual Morris water maze before nocturnal sleep; next-day spatial retrieval (dwell time in target area) tested sleep-dependent systems consolidation. LGI1 selectively targets DG and CA3.

Finding: Patients showed impaired sleep-associated consolidation of hippocampus-dependent spatial memory, consistent with DG/CA3 dysfunction disrupting pattern separation/completion during offline reprocessing. A human lesion-model corroboration that sleep consolidation of spatial memory depends on intact DG–CA3 circuitry.

⚠ Small clinical sample (n=15 patients); patient population limits causal generality, but theDG/CA3-selective pathology gives a clean natural experiment.

Individual differences in post-encoding sleep continuity predict context memory accuracy and supporting ERPs in younger and older adults
Nyan et al. (Mirjalili) · bioRxiv, 2026-07-06 Encoding-Retrieval Schema-Episodic

Approach: Actigraphy + EEG of naturalistic post-encoding sleep after encoding object–scene pairs; tested after a 96-hour sleep-filled delay. Relates sleep continuity to context-memory accuracy and retrieval-related ERPs in younger and older adults.

Finding: Greater post-encoding sleep continuity predicted better delayed context-memory performance for mismatching pairs and modulated the supporting ERP signatures, in both age groups. Extends sleep–memory work beyond deprivation labs to real-world sleep and ties consolidation to electrophysiological retrieval markers.

Visual recognition memory does not benefit from preserved temporal order from encoding to test
Zhao et al. (Woodman) · Attention, Perception, & Psychophysics, 2026 Encoding-Retrieval

Approach: Two experiments with a visual old/new recognition task; study items were re-presented at test either in the original study order or in a randomized order. Conditional-response-probability and two-alternative forced-choice analyses probed whether preserved temporal context aids retrieval.

Finding: Maintaining the original temporal order from encoding to test did not improve overall recognition accuracy, and temporal grouping from study order was unmodulated by test order. Challenges the assumption that temporal-context contiguity automatically confers a retrieval benefit in visual recognition.

Effects of spaced learning on knowledge acquisition, retention, and learning-related anxiety: a systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs
Sezgin et al. (Bektas) · Nurse Education in Practice, 2026 Encoding-Retrieval

Approach: Systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials examining spaced (distributed) vs. massed learning on knowledge acquisition, long-term retention, and anxiety in health-professions education.

Finding: Spaced learning improved knowledge acquisition and long-term retention and reduced learning-related anxiety relative to massed schedules. A high-quality synthesis confirming the spacing/lag effect at the level of durable memory consolidation, relevant to the spacing-effect literature in the keyword matrix.

Cross-cutting
The write-cost bottleneck: energy constraints on continual learning in brains and machines
Xu et al. (Yu) · Cognitive Neurodynamics, 2026 Cross-cutting

Approach: Theoretical analysis quantifying the metabolic “write” cost of learning (synaptic plasticity, receptor trafficking, protein synthesis, spine remodeling, consolidation) versus the better-studied “read” cost of signaling, estimating ~10^4–10^6 ATP per synapse per durable update.

Finding: Write costs, though a smaller time-averaged share of the mature brain's budget, form a peak-throughput bottleneck for any system that must learn continuously under fixed energy. Provides a normative energy argument constraining continual-learning architectures in both biological and artificial systems (relevant to catastrophic-forgetting / lifelong-learning work).

Auditory network persistence of stimulus representation in awake and naturally sleeping mice
Hadad et al. (Kimchy) · Communications Biology, 2026 Cross-cutting

Approach: Chronic high-density electrophysiology in freely behaving mice; time-resolved decoding of population spiking in the auditory cortical hierarchy after stimulus offset, compared across wakefulness and natural sleep.

Finding: Persistent population activity enabled decoding of past auditory stimuli after physical offset during both wake and natural sleep. In wake, representation decayed uniformly across sensory and association cortices; in sleep, association cortex sustained longer persistence. Documents a sleep-specific prolongation of stimulus representations relevant to predictive coding / working-memory-like persistence.
